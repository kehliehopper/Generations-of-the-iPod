• iPod Shuffle

    • The iPod Shuffle was a digital audio player designed and formerly marketed by Apple Inc. It was the smallest and most basic iPod and the only iPod with no screen, and was the first iPod to use flash memory. Introduced in 2005, the first generation was discontinued in 2006. Popular with runners and people on the go, unites clipped directly onto a sleeve or pocket. The First Shuffle plugged directly into the USB port. It was "autofilled" with 240 random songs from an iTunes playlist.

  • iPod Nano

    • The iPod Nano was a portable media player, designed and formerly marketed by Apple Inc. The first generation model was introducted in 2005, as a replacement for the iPod Shuffle, using flash memory for storage. The iPod Nano went through several different generations after its introduction. The Nane is considered a shrunken-down iPhone with 16GB of storage, a touchscreen, and a small home button. While it doesn't have a full app store, it offers much more than music. You can listen to podcasts, watch videos, and scroll through photos.

  • iPod Classic

    • The iPod Classic was a portable media player, created and formerly marketed by Apple Inc. There were six generations of the Ipod Classic, as well as a spin-off that was later re-intergrated into the main iPod line. This Classic was available in silver or black from 2007 onwards, replacing the "signiture iPod white." In 2014, Apple discontinued the iPod Classic. The sixth-generation 160GB iPod Classic was the last Apple product to use the original 30-pin dock connector and the distinctive Click Wheel.

  • iPod Touch

    • The iPod Touch is a music player and handhelf gaming device. It was marketed by Apple Inc. With a touchscreen-controlled user interface, it was also used as a digital camera, a web browser, and for messaging. It is very similar to the known iPhone, but it connects to the Internet only through Wi-Fi and does not use cellular network data, which is why it is not considered a smartphone. The iPod Touch was introduced in 2007 and by 2012, over 100 million units were sold. iPod touch models are sold by storage space and color. All models of the same generation typically offer identical features, performance, and operating systems.
