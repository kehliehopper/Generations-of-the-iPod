• iPod Shuffle

    • The iPod Shuffle was a digital audio player designed and formerly marketed by Apple Inc. It was the smallest and most basic iPod and the only iPod with no screen, and was the first iPod to use flash memory. Introduced in 2005, the shuffle was discontinued in 2017. Popular with runners and people on the go, unites clipped directly onto a sleeve or pocket. Starting with the 3rd-generation model, the VoiceOver text-to-speech feature audibly announced the names of songs, artists, and playlist.
